PART 1 — Diagram type and what it shows
- Type: architecture/system diagram.
- It shows structural layout drawings for a house:
  - a foundation plan with beams, girders, pads, slabs, and floor framing elements
  - a roof framing plan with rafters, ridge boards, ridge beams, ceiling joists, outriggers, and framing details
- Relationship type: compositional and hierarchical.
  - Smaller structural members compose larger assemblies.
  - Dimensions, notes, and callouts define how the parts fit together.

PART 3 — Structural insight
- The diagram is organized around the main structural load paths:
  - foundation support below
  - floor framing in the middle
  - roof framing above
- The most central elements are the beams, girders, ridge members, and joists, since they define how loads are carried through the structure.
- The foundation plan and roof framing plan are separate but related views of the same house structure.
- Removing a single labeled member would not erase the whole drawing, but it would remove part of the structural specification and could break the load path or make the plan incomplete.
- The diagram’s purpose is to communicate construction layout, member sizes, spacing, and support conditions for building the house structure.]
