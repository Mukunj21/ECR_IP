# Direct method vs Indirect method

[Figure: Comparison of cash flow statement presentation methods. Left side: Direct method with operating activities, investing activities, financing activities, and net movement in cash. Right side: Indirect method with net income, depreciation/amortization, working capital adjustments, and the same investing and financing sections.]

Direct method of cash flow starts with cash transactions.  
(Transactions are separated into cash received and cash paid.)

[Figure: Comparison of cash flow statement presentation methods. Left side: Direct method with operating activities, investing activities, financing activities, and net movement in cash. Right side: Indirect method with net income, depreciation/amortization, working capital adjustments, and the same investing and financing sections.]

Indirect method of cash flow starts with net income.  
(Non-cash adjustments are then added.)
