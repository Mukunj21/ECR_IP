## Diagram analysis

**Part 1 — Diagram type and meaning**

This is an **architecture/system diagram**: a **house floor plan**. It shows the spatial layout of rooms and building components in a residence, with dimension lines and annotations. The relationship type is primarily **compositional and hierarchical**: rooms, fixtures, doors, windows, and structural elements are arranged as parts of the overall house plan, with containment relationships between the house footprint and its interior spaces.

### Relationships

- The **house plan** contains the rooms/areas: **BEDROOM #1**, **BEDROOM #2**, **WALK IN CLOSET**, **ENTRY**, **LIVING ROOM**, **KITCHEN**, **DECK**, and **GARAGE**.
- **BEDROOM #2** is adjacent to **WALK IN CLOSET** and near a bathroom/service area.
- **BEDROOM #1** is adjacent to **ENTRY** and the central circulation area.
- **ENTRY** connects toward **LIVING ROOM**.
- **LIVING ROOM** connects to **KITCHEN** on the right and to the **DECK** above.
- **KITCHEN** connects to the **GARAGE** via the labeled **Solid-Core Self-Closing Door**.
- **GARAGE** contains/associates with **WH**, **Gas Furn**, **PP**, and diagonal brace notes.
- Window and door types are attached to wall segments, including **4040**, **4040T**, **6068T slider**, **2040**, and **GFI** locations.
- The **LEGEND** explains symbols used throughout the plan.
- The **GENERAL NOTES** block provides project notes and code/energy information.
- The plan is oriented by the **north arrow** marked **N**.
